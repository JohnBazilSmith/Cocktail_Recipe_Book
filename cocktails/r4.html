Negroni

The Negroni is an iconic Italian cocktail, famed for its perfect balance of bitter, sweet, and botanical notes.
Its vibrant red hue and sophisticated flavor profile make it a popular choice for an aperitif.
Originated in Italy, in 1919 when Count Camillo Negroni asked his bartender to strengthen his Americano by replacing the soda water with gin.

What do you need?

Ingredients:

  • 1 oz (30ml) Gin
  • 1 oz (30ml) Campari
  • 1 oz (30ml) Sweet Vermouth (preferably a quality Italian vermouth like Carpano Antica Formula or Martini Rosso)
  • Orange peel, for garnish

Tools:

  • Mixing glass
  • Bar spoon
  • Jigger (measuring tool)
  • Strainer
  • Old Fashioned glass (rocks glass)
  • Large ice cubes

Now how do you actually make it?

  1. Chill your glass: Place an Old Fashioned glass in the freezer or fill it with ice water to chill.
  2. Combine ingredients: In a mixing glass, add one large ice cube or several smaller ones. Pour in the gin, Campari, and sweet vermouth.
  3. Stir thoroughly: Using a bar spoon, stir the ingredients for about 20-30 seconds. This chills and slightly dilutes the cocktail, helping to meld the flavors.
  4. Strain: Remove the ice from your chilled Old Fashioned glass. Place a strainer over the mixing glass and strain the cocktail into the Old Fashioned glass over a large, fresh ice cube (or a few smaller ones).
  5. Garnish: Express the oils from an orange peel over the drink by twisting it, then drop the peel into the cocktail. Serve immediately.
